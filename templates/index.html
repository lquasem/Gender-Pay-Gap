WHAT IS THE GENDER PAY GAP?


The gender pay gap is the gap between what men and women are paid.
The gender pay gap or gender wage gap is the median difference between the remuneration for men and women who are working. Most commonly, it refers to the median annual pay of all women who work full time and year-round, compared to the pay of a similar cohort of men. Other estimates of the gender pay gap are based on weekly or hourly earnings, or are specific to a particular group of women.

Pay Gap by Country


The gender wage gap varies across countries. We took, 2016 data from 45 countries around the world and looked at the annual earnings of each gender and compared the differences. Our map illustrates the difference between the male & female earnings, so the thicker the blue border the larger the pay gap.For example, among OECD nations women in Australia, Belgium, Italy and Sweden earn 80% as much as males, whereas in Austria, Canada and Japan women earn about 60%.
