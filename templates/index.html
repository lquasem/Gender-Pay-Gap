EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

We have started analysing the data to find the gender pay gap, the difference between the earnings of women and men has shrunk over the years. But significant disparity in how men and women are paid still remains. The uncontrolled gender pay gap, which takes the ratio of median earnings of all women to all men, decreased by $0.05 since 2015. However, women still make only $0.79 for every dollar men make in 2019.




GENDER PAY GAP OVERALL


The Simple Truth about the Gender Pay Gap The gender pay gap is the gap between what men and women are paid.
Most commonly, it refers to the median annual pay of all women who work full time and year-round, compared to the pay of a similar cohort of men. Other estimates of the gender pay gap are based on weekly or hourly earnings, or are specific to a particular group of women.
